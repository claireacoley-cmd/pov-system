Why this exists

I had five tools full of documents, screenshots and ideas but was struggling to put together a clear point of view.

The system looked like a Notion with old stories, Pinterest boards of cool visuals, Docs of old frameworks mashed alongside ideas that never made it online. More recently, I had a new challenge...the stuff stored in my various AI chats, playing host to some mix of personal experience with whatever topics I found interesting that week.

Nothing was lost, but I didn't know how to find (or use) any of it.

You might know what I mean.

You sit down to write and need the thing that proves your belief about differentiation. You know it's there because you remember writing it down whilst listening to a podcast making dinner one night. Eventually, you find it nested against recipes for mushroom stroganoff, a family birthday and bullets prepping a performance review. It's a half-baked idea you were meant to come back to and flesh out, to add a story of "that time" this thing happened to you IRL. And now? You have no idea why you thought this was worth saving.

You decide to sort your reference system out once and for all and spend the weekend doing it. But it doesn't fix the problem. Because everything's organised by where it came from or when you saved it, and nothing's organised by why it matters. You can't work out why none of it matches how you think.

The question that changes this is a small one.

Instead of "what is this?" you ask: why do I want to save this? Is it something I believe — and how much? Would I go to war for this idea, or is it more of a polite nod?

Perhaps it's not a belief at all. Maybe it's an interesting example, a story that shaped a bigger belief, or a moment that changed how you think. A proof point.

Or it could be something you just admire, a brand you wish you'd built, a hook you saved "just in case", a landing page that made you buy. Something that made you feel and needed a place to reference later.

The reality is, a belief, a personal story and a cool brand are not doing the same thing… but they're connected. Think nested instead of rubbing shoulders, but to notice these connections, you have to see the pieces in the same place.

That's what the POV System does.

Your beliefs sit at the centre. Proof connects them to the evidence: your stories, other people's stories, examples, patterns, data. Craft references sit alongside — the formats, structures and turns of phrase you want to reach for when you're expressing yourself.

When you sit down to write or refine a strategy, you pull from your own accumulated thinking. You see what you've already gathered. You build and riff on what you already have, instead of starting over.

Beliefs come from proof and show up in the world via craft. And now they're back together where they belong.

— Claire

How it works
Three types of material
Belief Your positions — what you think is true and why. Status moves from Exploring → Developing → Conviction. The spine everything else hangs from.
Proof Evidence. Stories, examples, patterns, data, perspectives. Six source types so you can see where the evidence is thin.
Craft How to express it. Format, visual, tone. Things you've noticed about how others deliver ideas well.

Think button
↓
For ideas that arrive messy. Dump what you've got — raw, unfiltered. Claude identifies the threads, spars with you until it resolves, then proposes a filing brief. You approve. Items land in the right places.

Navigating

The Inbox holds things in progress — sparring sessions, half-filed ideas. Open one to continue the conversation or send it through.

Click any belief in the sidebar to pull up everything connected to it: proof that supports it, craft that would help you express it.

Filters at the top cut by function, domain, or proof source type. Search reaches inside notes and why-saved fields.
